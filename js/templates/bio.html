Bio

Alfonso (Al) Guilin was born in Yorba Linda, California, March 24, 1938 to Leopoldo and Vicenta Guilin. His family moved to Blythe, California where he attended Palo Verde High School. He graduated from California State University Pomona in 1961 with a B.S. degree in Agricultural Business Management. He attended the Anderson School of Business Management program at UCLA. His career began in agricultural supply sales, and the State Farm Labor Service. In 1965 he began a thirty year career with the Limoneira Company in Santa Paula, California. Rising from personnel management to Executive Vice President, a position he held for the last 15 years of his association with the Company. Under his leadership the Limoneira Federal Credit Union was founded as well as the Limoneira Scholarship Fund. He is a Kellogg Fellow and member of Class I of the Agricultural Leadership Association which was founded in 1970. This program allowed him to travel extensively in the United States in order to expand knowledge of domestic farming in the United States. The following year was dedicated to travel in Europe to observe the various types of agriculture in the European Common Market.

Al also served as a member and President of the California Avocado Commission and was sent on a fact finding mission to Mexico prior to the importation of avocados into the United States. Called on by ACDI/VOCA, a private non-profit organization that promotes broad based economic growth in low income and emerging countries, he traveled to the Republic of Georgia to observe and advocate for the advancement of agribusiness in that country.

During this time he served on many boards including, Agricultural Producers, Santa Paula Savings and Loan, Briggs School Board of Trustees, The Ventura County Symphony. He was a founding director of the Destino 2000 Fund of The Ventura County Community Foundation. He was the President of the Rotary Club of Santa Paula in 1973. Due to his support of Republican Hispanic activities he was invited to the White House for luncheon with President Ronald Reagan. Al was named the Distinguished Alumnus of the College of Agriculture at Cal Poly Pomona in 1993.

After his retirement from the Limoneira Company in 1996, he began serving in volunteer activities with his church. Feeling the call to ordained ministry he entered studies and was ordained a Permanent Deacon in the Archdiocese of Los Angeles in 2001. He currently serves as Deacon at St. Sebastian and Our Lady of Guadalupe Churches. He is actively involved in the Society of St. Vincent de Paul where he serves as Spiritual Director.

He also has authored five books about the agricultural community drawing from his lifelong relationship both from a personal perspective with the people who do the work and also the business sides of the industry.

He continues to serve his community in his many civic involvements including; The Santa Paula Chamber of Commerce, The Fireworks Committee, the Farm Worker Housing Committee, the Labor Day Parade Committee, the Santa Paula Ministerial Association, the Santa Paula Fire and Police Association and many other activities as he is called on.
